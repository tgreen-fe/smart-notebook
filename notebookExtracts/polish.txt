The history of the Kraków Ghetto
The exhibition was opened on the 80th anniversary of the liquidation of the ghetto. It was created in memory of the Jews of Kraków who were forc
by the Germans to leave their homes and move to the ghetto established in Podgórze. From there, the vast majority were deported to concentration an
camps.
There is still a myth in the popular consciousness that Jews during the Holocaust behaved passively, did not fight or put up any resistance. In this exhibition
we try to show how untrue and harmful such statements are.
Resistance did not always have to mean fighting with weapons. Many of the thousands of Jews imprisoned in the Kraków Ghetto, as in other ghettos in occu-
ied Poland, began their private struggle against the Nazis and their collaborators by trying to preserve their own dignity. Even a small gesture towards another
rson or an ordinary, everyday activity performed despite everything, against hopelessness and resignation, could be an act of resistance.
